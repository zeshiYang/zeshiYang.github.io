Zeshi Yang (楊 澤世)

I am an independent researcher working on humanoid intelligence and decision-making, with the goal of building physically grounded and emotionally rich agents that can act and interact naturally in the world. My research is guided more by problems than by methodological boundaries: I draw on principle-based reasoning, planning, and data-driven learning as needed to tackle challenging problems in control, behavior generation, and interaction. I have a lot of experience at realizing agile and dexterous motions on simulated humanoids. I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from Simon Fraser University in 2023, following a bachelor's degree in Applied Physics from USTC in 2018.
